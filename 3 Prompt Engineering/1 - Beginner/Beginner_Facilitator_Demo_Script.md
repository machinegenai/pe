# Beginner Course — Facilitator Demo Script

**Course:** Prompt Engineering, Level 1 — *From Asking to Composing* · **Length:** ~45 min · **Tool for demos:** Claude (works the same in Copilot / ChatGPT)

This is your copy-paste sheet for the live session. Keep it open beside the deck. Every prompt below is ready to paste straight into Claude. Run the three demos **in the same chat**, in order, so the Refine demo builds on the CO-STAR one.

> ⚠️ **Before you run this for real:** the membership renewal/refund details below are **illustrative examples**, not confirmed BJ's policy. Replace them with BJ's actual renewal and refund policy before using any output with a real member.

## Demo 1 — BEFORE (the one-line prompt) · slide 7

**Paste into Claude:**

```
Write a reply to this member:

"BJ's charged me $60 to auto-renew my membership. Nobody warned me. I want my money back."
```

**What to say:** *"Polite, but useless — no empathy, no explanation, no options, no next step. Let's fix the prompt, not the tool."*

---

## Demo 2 — AFTER (built with CO-STAR) · slide 10

**Best delivery:** build it live. Start from the one-liner and add each part out loud — Context, then Objective, then Style, Tone, Audience, Response — re-running as you go so the room sees the answer sharpen. If you're short on time, paste the whole thing at once.

**Paste into Claude (the full CO-STAR prompt):**

```
Context: I work in Member Care at BJ's Wholesale Club. A member is upset that their membership auto-renewed — a $60 charge they say they weren't warned about. Our policy: a member can get a full refund of the renewal fee within 30 days if they haven't used the membership since it renewed, and we email a renewal reminder about 30 days before the charge.

Objective: Write a reply that acknowledges their frustration, gently explains that we send a reminder before renewal, and lays out the options available to them.

Style: Plain language, short paragraphs, no corporate jargon.

Tone: Warm and empathetic — never defensive.

Audience: A frustrated member who feels blindsided and wants their money back.

Response: An email of 120–150 words. Open with a genuine acknowledgement, give the two options (refund if eligible, or keep the membership with a quick note on the value), and end with a clear next step and a warm sign-off.
```

**What to say:** *"Same tool, same member, about a minute more effort — and now it's actually usable."*

---

## Demo 3 — REFINE (one follow-up) · slide 13

**In the same chat, paste:**

```
Make it a little warmer, keep it under 120 words, and offer a one-time $20 courtesy credit if they'd like to stay.
```

**What to say:** *"I didn't start over — I nudged it. It kept everything good and changed only what I asked. That's the Refine habit."*

## If the live tool fails
Every demo's expected output is printed on the slide (the green box). You can teach the entire before → after → refine story straight from the deck with zero connectivity. Narrate the green boxes and keep moving.

## The one card to leave them with
**C**ontext · **O**bjective · **S**tyle · **T**one · **A**udience · **R**esponse — then **↻ Refine** (check it, improve in one follow-up). It's slide 15, and it's the thing they'll teach their team.
